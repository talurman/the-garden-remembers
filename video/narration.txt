This is The Garden Remembers, a first-person experience where a place notices what happened without turning it into a score.

The first encounter begins with a lost wallet. The player can approach different possibilities, ask for context, or keep exploring. Every decision receives an equally complete response.

The consequence does not end at the prompt. It moves through water, paper, flowers, and the path, then returns later as memory.

At the paper threshold, the player can listen, knock, use the call point, or continue onward. The garden records only observable events and uses them to build its final reflection.

I used Codex to develop, test, debug, and prepare the release. GPT-5.6 helped reason across every branch and translate the non-judgmental design rules into automated checks.

The result is a garden that remembers your presence without declaring a correct ending.
