# The Garden Remembers — World & Interaction Redesign

## The new promise

The garden is not a neutral level containing four choices. It is a living instrument. Every encounter has its own spatial grammar, and every decision leaves a material memory that becomes part of the remaining walk.

The emotional curve is **curiosity → unease → care → social recognition → self-recognition**. The distant moon gate stays visible often enough to orient the player, but the route repeatedly folds, narrows and opens so wandering remains possible.

## World rules

1. **Ivory is silence, color is memory.** Turquoise, coral, gold and indigo enter through action and remain in restrained traces.
2. **Guidance belongs to matter.** Water, fabric, petals, seams, sound and light guide the player. UI only confirms an action at close range.
3. **Every interaction has three beats.** Notice → commit → witness. The player must see both the local human response and the wider environmental response.
4. **No reset between encounters.** Completed encounters continue breathing at a low level and recolor the downstream path.
5. **Different situations need different silhouettes.** A player should recognize an encounter before reading any words.

## Encounter identities

### The Wallet — five-petal clearing

- **Question:** What do I do with something that is not mine?
- **Before:** the orange wallet is the only saturated object; five possible responses exist as distant, readable landmarks.
- **Commit:** carrying the wallet physically connects the center to the selected landmark.
- **After:** pigment runs outward like a coral thread, touches water and planting, then reappears later along the route.
- **Improvement:** keep the contemporary visible-faced visitor. Remove any hood/robe cues and avoid presenting all five actions as equivalent UI buttons.

### The Threshold — compressed vertical seam

- **Question:** How do I respond when I cannot see the other side?
- **Before:** the path narrows, sound becomes directional and a warm seam is visible from far away.
- **Commit:** knock, listen and call each move a different physical object; leaving is performed by walking onward.
- **After:** the architecture inhales and releases. The chosen sound becomes a faint gold resonance in later metal and water.

### Quiet Shelter — low protective canopy

- **Question:** How close do I come to someone who has not asked?
- **Before:** textile, basin and bench form a low, intimate hollow; the person's breathing subtly moves the entire shelter.
- **Commit:** actions are staged through spatial objects—seat, hand, water, call point—or by increasing distance.
- **After:** the response is small and credible. Water and fabric carry the memory; the person is never treated as a reward animation.

### The Circle — broken social ring

- **Question:** What do I do with group pressure?
- **Before:** bodies and cloth form a readable inward spiral with one broken interval for the player.
- **Commit:** stepping in, joining and distracting change the player's physical position; calling travels as a visible line; leaving remains locomotion.
- **After:** spacing, gaze and the ring geometry reorganize. The downstream court remembers the new interval rather than labelling the choice.

## Whole-world accumulation

- **0 memories:** sage/ivory, cool water, low wind, sparse movement.
- **1 memory:** local color follows footsteps; one distant harmonic appears.
- **2 memories:** water carries two-color caustics; paper screens answer at longer range.
- **3 memories:** coral and turquoise cross between encounter sites; the gate becomes warmer and more dimensional.
- **4 memories:** the garden reaches its fullest color, but remains luminous and quiet rather than becoming neon.

## Production test

At any point, a still frame should answer three questions without UI: **Where might I go? What kind of situation is ahead? What has this garden remembered so far?**
