## Objective

Transform the original second booth, **The Hallway**, into the next continuous garden encounter without discarding its strongest behavior:

> Approach → hear more → hesitate or investigate → act or leave → the threshold remembers the sequence.

The encounter must remain understandable as a situation, not a psychological test. It must never claim that knocking, listening, calling, or leaving reveals a trait or represents the morally correct response.

## Art Bible V4 prerequisite

`ART_BIBLE_V4_CHARACTER_OBJECT_STANDARD.md` is the visual source of truth for this encounter. The new character and wallet-encounter cuts establish a stricter production language than the current prototype.

Before threshold scene production begins, the existing wallet encounter must receive a visual-convergence pass:

- Replace the rounded, cartoon-like crying figure with the Art Bible's slender indigo-robed human silhouette.
- Express the figure's before/after state through posture, gaze, hand gesture and reflected environmental color; remove visible tear drops, facial caricature and head bobbing.
- Bring the wallet, notice area, call point, bench/fabric and return place into one limestone, paper, wood, ceramic, textile and aged-metal material family.
- Replace identical sparkle/halo treatment with equally strong but object-specific physical cues.
- Preserve all existing wallet choices, prompts, consequences, memory facts and tests while changing their real-time presentation.
- Verify the wallet response changes the same composition—character, object, path, water, plants, paper, props and light—without swapping props or layouts.

This is not a general polish task. The current procedural character construction is the wrong shape language and must be replaced before it becomes the template for later human figures.

## Original interaction contract

The original booth contains four meaningful actions:

- Knock on the door.
- Listen near the door.
- Call emergency services.
- Leave.

Its strongest existing ideas are:

- Sound intensity changes with distance from the door.
- The player must physically approach before choosing.
- Knocking and listening do not end the situation; the player can continue investigating.
- Calling and leaving resolve the local sequence.
- Keyboard and pointer controls both operate the interaction.

Its weakest elements are:

- A dark, isolated hallway that breaks continuity with the garden.
- A four-card menu that feels like a moral questionnaire.
- A full-screen phone animation.
- Cartoon police figures and a generic siren.
- Judgmental ending copy such as “reported in time.”
- No persistent consequence after returning to the garden.

## Creative direction

### The place

The player simply continues walking out of the wallet garden and enters a narrower garden passage. A pale limestone wall bends across the route. Set slightly off-axis within it is a tall handmade-paper door. The moon gate remains farther ahead as the final reflection point, not as a divider between encounters.

The place remains luminous:

- Mist-blue daylight and pale ivory plaster.
- A restrained indigo seam around the door.
- Sage planting and shallow water continuing beside the passage.
- Warm mineral light leaking beneath the threshold.
- Bamboo, fabric and paper responding to sound.

The passage should feel quieter and more compressed than the wallet garden, but never dark or horror-coded. A slit beside the wall preserves a view of distant mountains so the player never feels trapped.

### Composition rules

- Keep the door off-center and reveal it through a bend.
- Use foreground bamboo or fabric to frame the first view.
- Preserve a clear route past the threshold so leaving is always physically legible.
- Place the call box in a shallow wall niche, not as another freestanding phone booth.
- Keep the middle of the passage open; do not decorate every surface.
- Use sound, paper vibration and light direction—not objective markers—to draw attention.

## Interaction grammar

### Discovery

1. The player first notices a slight vibration in nearby paper and fabric.
2. A spatial sound becomes clearer as distance to the door decreases.
3. The warm line beneath the threshold widens subtly when the player faces the door.
4. No text appears until the player enters the close interaction radius.

### Contextual prompt

At close range, show one temporary prompt:

**Voices behind the door. What do you do?**

- Knock.
- Listen.
- Use the call box.
- Keep walking.

This retains the understandable interaction style the wallet established. Arrow keys, Enter, pointer and touch must have full parity. Escape and “Keep walking” close the prompt and immediately return movement.

### Multi-action structure

Knocking and listening are **non-terminal**. The player may:

- Knock once or several times.
- Listen before or after knocking.
- Step away and return.
- Call after gathering information.
- Leave after knocking or listening.
- Pass without opening the prompt.

Calling or crossing the downstream exit boundary closes the local sequence. No action is described as success or failure.

## Observable behavior

Use the existing factual vocabulary whenever possible:

- `location.entered` and `location.exited` for `threshold-place`.
- `movement.approached`, `movement.retreated`, `movement.backtracked` and `movement.still`.
- `attention.oriented` or `attention.looked` toward `paper-door`, with duration for sustained listening.
- `interaction.started`, `interaction.interrupted` and `interaction.completed` with factual action metadata.
- `environment.contact` for the knocker, door, call box and downstream paper.
- `threshold.crossed` when the player continues beyond the wall.

Do not add fields for courage, empathy, suspicion, responsibility, anxiety or morality.

## Outcome and memory contract

Each route receives equal production quality. The final outcome selects the primary palette and delayed motif; earlier knocks, listening duration and returns become factual modifiers rather than separate combinatorial endings.

| Route | Immediate physical response | Palette | Delayed memory |
|---|---|---|---|
| Knock | Knocker moves; three pressure rings travel through paper and plaster; the sound pauses, then resumes | Clay coral and restrained indigo | The knock rhythm returns later as three bamboo or stepping-stone pulses |
| Listen | The sound filter opens briefly; paper fibers tighten; water beside the wall carries fine concentric lines | Mist blue and pale turquoise | Downstream paper lifts in the same cadence as the listening duration |
| Use call box | Receiver lifts; a short distant acknowledgement answers; a signal travels along the wall and water | Indigo and warm gold | Paired indigo/gold light reaches the next canopy and pavilion paper |
| Keep walking | Fabric settles across the niche; the sound becomes distant while the route remains open | Sage and warm ivory | A quiet closed-paper motif appears later without looking incomplete or punished |

### Sequence modifiers

Do not create a unique authored ending for every possible order. Store a compact factual summary:

- Number of knocks.
- Total close listening duration.
- Number of approaches and retreats.
- Whether the call box was used.
- Whether the player returned after leaving the close radius.
- Whether the player passed without opening the prompt.

Use at most one secondary modifier in the delayed echo. Example: a call outcome keeps its indigo/gold signal, while three faint plaster rings preserve the fact that the player knocked first.

## Return state

Returning to the threshold must reveal a stable consequence:

- After knocking, the knocker remains slightly displaced and the wall retains faint pressure rings.
- After listening, the paper seam reacts sooner to the returning player.
- After using the call box, an indigo/gold thread remains between the niche and downstream paper.
- After leaving, the threshold remains quiet and luminous; it must not look failed or unfinished.

Return recognition must never invent what happened behind the door. The garden remembers the player’s behavior, not the unseen outcome.

## Reflection language

The observation layer stores only verifiable behavior: movement shape, pauses, orientation, approach and retreat, sequence, completion and return. At the final gate, those signals are combined into one nuanced personality portrait rather than replayed as an action log.

The portrait may describe tendencies such as exploratory, reflective, self-directed or responsive only as a tentative pattern. It must not expose numerical dimensions, claim certainty, diagnose a condition, infer motive, assign morality or claim what happened behind the door.

## Stage-completion gate

The second interaction is complete only when:

1. All four original actions remain available.
2. Knock and listen can occur in multiple orders without forcing resolution.
3. Leaving is valid both through the prompt and through physical movement.
4. Every final route creates an immediate, propagated, delayed and return state.
5. The threshold stays understandable with audio muted.
6. Wallet memory survives into the second garden chapter.
7. Reflection synthesizes factual evidence into a nuanced, non-diagnostic personality paragraph across both encounters.
8. A fresh player independently notices that the threshold remembers at least one earlier behavior.

Only after this gate passes should the third original interaction begin.
