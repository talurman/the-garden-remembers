# Demo video plan — target 2:35

Keep the final export below three minutes. Record the voiceover; remove typing, loading, and long walking stretches. It is fine to speed up travel footage while keeping interaction moments at normal speed.

## 0:00–0:15 — Title and premise

**Visual:** Title screen, then enter the garden.

**Voiceover:** “This is The Garden Remembers, a first-person experience about a place that notices how you move through it. It remembers observable actions, but it never turns them into a score, diagnosis, or good and bad ending.”

## 0:15–0:55 — Exploration and wallet encounter

**Visual:** Walk through the garden, show pigment footsteps, approach the wallet, and briefly reveal the spatial options.

**Voiceover:** “I move freely through a continuous Three.js garden. My footsteps leave pigment, and encounters are discovered through space rather than a quiz. Here, finding the wallet opens five possibilities around the environment. Approaching one gives me context, but I can always keep exploring.”

## 0:55–1:30 — Memory loop

**Visual:** Choose one wallet route, show its physical consequence, then cut to the delayed response elsewhere.

**Voiceover:** “When I choose, the garden records only what happened: where I approached, what I carried or placed, whether I left, and whether I returned. The response has four layers—an immediate echo, a delayed change after I move on, recognition if I return, and a convergent memory near the ending. Every route gets a complete response, so beauty is never used as a reward for the ‘right’ answer.”

## 1:30–1:55 — Threshold and reflection

**Visual:** Show the threshold choices, then cut to the final paper reflection.

**Voiceover:** “The threshold supports listening, knocking, calling, or simply walking on. The final reflection is deterministic and built from observations the garden actually recorded. The underlying schema rejects personality traits, empathy scores, anxiety labels, diagnoses, and other unsupported inferences.”

## 1:55–2:25 — Codex and GPT-5.6

**Visual:** Show the module folders, a brief Codex session shot, and the passing test output. Make the words ‘Codex’, ‘GPT-5.6’, and ‘58 tests passing’ readable on screen.

**Voiceover:** “I used Codex as the active development workspace to audit the original prototype, refactor the memory architecture, implement and debug the continuous experience, and run regression checks. GPT-5.6 helped me reason across the whole system—comparing every branch for equal depth, finding state and input edge cases, and turning the non-judgmental design rules into automated tests. I kept the creative direction human-led and verified the work with 58 passing tests and clean browser runs.”

## 2:25–2:35 — Close

**Visual:** A strong garden or reflection shot with the project title.

**Voiceover:** “The result is a garden that does not tell you who you are. It simply shows that your presence left a trace.”

## Final video QA

- Voiceover is present and clearly audible over the game sound.
- “Codex” and “GPT-5.6” are both said out loud.
- Runtime interaction is shown, not only slides or code.
- No loading, typing, or silent travel remains.
- Duration is under 3:00.
- YouTube visibility is Public or Unlisted, never Private.
- The public YouTube link opens in a signed-out/private window.
