## Purpose

The Circle adapts the original bullying booth into the continuous garden. A smaller figure stands inside a loose ring of older figures. Pointing, posture, spacing and fragmented voices make the social situation legible before any text appears.

It sits on the right side of the path, opposite the Quiet Shelter. Both situations become visible from the same approach line. The garden observes which situation the player notices and approaches first, but never treats that first movement as a moral answer or a personality result by itself.

## Spatial relationship with the Quiet Shelter

- Keep the Quiet Shelter on the left near its current `z` position.
- Place The Circle on the right at approximately the same journey depth, using the path as shared negative space between them.
- Keep the moon gate visible beyond both situations.
- Preserve a wide central route so the player may approach either side, look between them, visit both, or continue.
- Give both encounters comparable approach distance and visual exposure.
- Because the circle naturally contains more bodies and sound, set it slightly farther from the path and keep its voices restrained so it does not automatically dominate the seated person.
- Do not add signs, quest markers, arrows or explanatory labels.

## Step 5 — Implement five equal physical sequences

Every direct route follows:

> attention → physical cause → group response → control returns → persistent trace

### Step between them

- The player crosses the social boundary with a restrained hand/stance gesture.
- Nearby figures shift weight and the ring opens asymmetrically.
- The central route becomes physically clearer without claiming the person was rescued.

### Join their pointing

- The player moves into an available gap and briefly aligns with the group’s orientation.
- The ring includes the player’s position and its paper shadows tighten.
- Do not display condemnation or say the player became “part of the problem.”

### Draw their attention away

- A credible player gesture or nearby fabric/wood contact creates the cause.
- Group gaze and cloth turn outward toward the player.
- The central figure gains spatial room, but the game does not claim what they do next.

### Call to the person in the center

- Use a restrained open-hand and voice gesture.
- The central figure turns or acknowledges the sound without being forced to approach.
- A path of warm reflected light becomes visible between the two positions.

### Continue onward

- Close the prompt and preserve movement.
- Resolve only after the player physically leaves the dual-encounter field downstream.
- Let the circle remain present and settle into a complete neutral memory state.

Acceptance:

- Every route has comparable duration, physical clarity, sound and environmental consequence.
- No character performs gratitude, punishment, humiliation or a guaranteed outcome.
- Every endpoint leaves a valid walking route.
