# Rejected Iteration — The Divided House Rooms

Status: removed from `index.html`.

The two-room translation was technically functional but conceptually wrong. It turned an encounter into an optional destination, reduced its human context, and interrupted the continuous journey. It is preserved here only as a record of what not to carry forward.

## Preserved dramatic DNA

The original fifth pavilion asked the player to move down a corridor and embody a spatial decision between two rooms. The redesign preserves movement, hesitation, side preference and the possibility of remaining between spaces.

It removes the countdown, isolated iframe, forced ending, explicit instruction and warm-versus-modern value framing.

## Why the implementation was removed

- It required the player to leave the journey and enter an authored destination.
- The warm/cool contrast communicated aesthetic preference more strongly than a human situation.
- The rooms provided less narrative information than the original pavilion content.
- The garden reacted to a zone, but the larger world did not feel as though it grew from the player's action.
- Technical correctness did not compensate for the weaker emotional premise.

## Replacement direction

Preserve only the original behavioral insight—following one direction, changing course, exploring alternatives or continuing—inside a path-native human situation. Do not rebuild literal side rooms.
